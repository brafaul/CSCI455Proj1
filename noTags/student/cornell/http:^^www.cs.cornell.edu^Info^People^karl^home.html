(TU Karlsruhe, 1990) Graduate Student Cornell University Dept. of Computer Science 4157 Upson Hall Ithaca, NY 14853, USA Tel: (607) 255-1164 Lab: (607) 255-2329 ext. 501 Fax: (607) 255-4428 Email: karl@cs.cornell.edu My current research interests are micro robotics, manipulation, and assembly. At the Cornell Nanofabrication Facility I am building microfabricated actuator arrays that implement micro manipulation strategies. More generally I am interested in new devices for handling and assembling parts, and manipulation strategies with programmable force vector fields. I have also investigated design automation for micro structures. Earlier work at the University of Karlsruhe, Germany, has included the development of better graph layout algorithms. My thesis advisor is Professor Bruce Donald, who is one of the founders and directors of the Cornell Computer Science Robotics and Vision Laboratory. My project is in close collaboration with Professor Noel MacDonald and his research group. Publications and Other Documents. Conference Announcement and Call for Papers.
