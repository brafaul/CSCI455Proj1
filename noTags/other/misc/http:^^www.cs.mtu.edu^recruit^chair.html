The successful candidate will provide leadership for the interdisciplinary Computational Science and Engineering (CS&E) doctoral program and should have a long term commitment to furthering the overall development of the Department. The candidate is also expected to represent the Department in interdisciplinary computational activities throughout Michigan Tech. The Department of Computer Science currently has ten faculty positions. Faculty conduct research in artificial intelligence, communication algorithms, compiler optimization, computational biology, computational geometry, computer graphics, geometric/solid modeling, interconnection networks, parallel architectures, parallel processing, performance evaluation, sequence comparison algorithms, software development environments, and software measurement. The Department has an active undergraduate program with 280 majors, an M.S. program with 30 students, and a newly established CS&E Ph.D. program. Departmental computing facilities consist of networks of Sun workstations, Dec Alpha's, SGI's, and X-terminals. Computer Science operates three instructional Sun workstation labs. Michigan Tech, designated as one of four Michigan research universities, has 6,200 students and 400 faculty. The University is located in the scenic Keweenaw Peninsula of Michigan's Upper Peninsula. Surrounded by the clean water of Lake Superior and nearby forests, the community offers year-round recreational and cultural enrichment opportunities. Applications, nominations, and inquiries should be sent to: Dr. John Lowther Search Committee Chair Department of Computer Science Michigan Technological University 1400 Townsend Drive Houghton, MI 49931-1295 Phone: (906) 487-2209 Email: john@mtu.edu Fax: (906) 487-2283 Information about the Department is available at http://www.cs.mtu.edu/. Applications should include a detailed resume and the names and addresses of at least four references. The search committee will review applications on a continuing basis and will accept applications until the position is filled. Applications from minority and women candidates are strongly encouraged. Michigan Technological University is an Equal Opportunity Educational Institution/Equal Opportunity Employer.
