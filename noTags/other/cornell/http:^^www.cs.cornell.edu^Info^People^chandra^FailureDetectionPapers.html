Unreliable failure detectors for Reliable Distributed Systems introduces failure detectors for message passing systems (To appear in JACM). By Tushar Deepak Chandra and Sam Toueg. The weakest failure detector for solving consensus: determines what information about failures is necessary and sufficient to solve Consensus in asynchronous distributed systems (message passing) subject to crash failures (a preliminary version of this paper appeared in PODC92). By Tushar Deepak Chandra, Vassos Hadzilacos, and Sam Toueg. Tushar Deepak Chandra's thesis combines the results in Unreliable failure detectors for Reliable Distributed Systems and the weakest failure detector for solving consensus. Using Failure Detectors to Solve Consensus in Asynchronous Shared-Memory Systems extends the work mentioned above to shared-memory systems with registers. This paper also contains results on resource complexity that have no analogue in the work mentioned above (a preliminary version of this paper appeared in WDAG94). By Wai Kao Lo and Vassos Hadzilacos. Failure Detectors and the Wait-Free hierarchy extends the work on shared-memory systems with registers to shared-memory systems with arbitrary objects (a preliminary version of this paper appeared in PODC96). By Gil Neiger. Simulating Fail-Stop in Asynchronous Distributed Systems shows that it is possible to specify and implement a failure model that is indistinguishable from the fail-stop model from the point of view of any process within an asynchronous system. By Laura Sabel and Keith Marzullo. Election Vs. Consensus in Asynchronous Systems. By Laura Sabel and Keith Marzullo. Revisiting the relationship between Non Blocking Atomic Commitment and Consensus problems. By R. Guerraoui. Non-Blocking Atomic Commitment with an Unreliable Failure Detector. By R. Guerraoui, Miquel Larrea, and A. Schiper Gamma-Accurate failure detectors. By R. Guerraoui and A. Schiper The Decentralized Non Blocking Atomic Commitment Protocol. By R. Guerraoui and A.
